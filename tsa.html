Leadership and Academics

I am the current Delaware TSA State Treasurer, and I am the Chairman of DETSA's Leadership Committee. This year's goal for the Leadership Committee is to increase active involvement of chapters and advisors. We accomplished this by hosting the Fall Leadership Conference where middle schoolers and high schoolers created work plans. This was the first time that high school students created work plans for their chapters. We also hosted DETSA's largest-ever Winter Leadership Lunch, which was attended by students seeking leadership positions in Delaware TSA, including many of the officer candidates before you today. Both of these events helped to increase the quality of our current and future leaders, helping to strengthen Delaware TSA.

In addition to this, I receive straight A's in all of my classes, including AP Calculus BC, AP Biology, and AP World History. I also create study guides for many of my classes, which are received by about 80% of the people in my grade taking those classes. You can view my study guides at cwagaman.com.

Activities

I do many other activities in addition to TSA. I am a member of the Varsity Cross Country team and the Varsity Track team (I run long-distance). I have also won back-to-back state championships in Academic WorldQuest, a current events competition. We will be attending Nationals next week, and last year we placed 16th at Nationals. You can see my team with Governor Jack Markell in the picture.

I am also founder and Editor-in-Chief of my school's news website, ConcordHS.xyz. This experience will help me to improve DETSA's visibility so that more people know what TSA is and what we do. In my free time, I volunteer and I play Ultimate Frisbee.

Ideas

Few people know what TSA is. Even fewer people know what we do. As state officer, I would improve visibility of Delaware TSA in the media, at school, and in everyday life. I will also create more effective methods for schools to spread the word about TSA. This will lead to membership growth, and as a result TSA will grow stronger at the chapter, state, and even national level. Also, if more people know what TSA is, being part of it will have a higher value, and college admissions officers will see it as a greater asset. Like any other organization, TSA needs government funding, and if more people know all that we do for the community, its funding will be more likely to continue for years to come.
