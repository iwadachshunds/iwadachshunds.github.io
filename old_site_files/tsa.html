Leadership and Academics

I am the current Delaware TSA State Reporter, and I am the Chairman of DETSA's Communication Committee. This year, the Communication Committee increased news posts by 50% from last year, hitting a new record for number of news posts under a State Reporter. Last year, as Leadership Committee Chairman, we increased active involvement of chapters and advisors by hosting the Fall Leadership Conference where middle schoolers and high schoolers created work plans. That was the first time that high school students created work plans for their chapters, and the return of the work plan creation this year was a result of the great success we experienced then. Both of these committees have helped to increase the quality of our current and future leaders, helping to strengthen Delaware TSA.

In addition to this, I receive straight A's in all of my classes, including my 5 AP classes. I also create study guides for some of my classes, which are received by about 70% of the people in my grade taking those classes. You can view my study guides at cwagaman.com.

Activities

I do many other activities in addition to TSA. I am captain of the Varsity Cross Country team, where I earned All-Conference and All-County honors, and I am a state-qualifier on the Varsity Track team (I run long-distance). I have also won three consecutive state championships in Academic WorldQuest, a current events competition. We will be attending Nationals later this week, and last year we placed 6th at Nationals. You can see my team with Governor Jack Markell in the picture.

I am also Vice President of my school's Academic Bowl team, which this year qualified for the national competition for the first time in school history! In my free time, I volunteer, and sometimes I play Ultimate Frisbee.

Ideas

TSA is a great organization with a huge membership. However, through better communication with members and chapters, in combination with other programs, we can increase unity statewide. This will allow us to better-leverage our great membership. Additionally, by continuing and expanding the Friends of Delaware TSA program (which increases awareness and outreach for local businesses), we can continue to increase recognition for Delaware TSA in our community. Like any other organization, TSA needs funding, and if more people know all that we do for the community, and if we as an organization do more for the community through increased unity, its funding will be more likely to continue for years to come, and our members will be more successful.
